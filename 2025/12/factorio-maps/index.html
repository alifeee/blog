What's this about?

Last year I organised a playthrough of Factorio with my friends. The invite read:

Let's play a big Factorio multiplayer game!
ðŸ•’3 hours every ðŸ“…Tuesday evening at ðŸ•–7pm, first session ðŸ“…9th January
My plan is that once a week, I will host a server for three hours. So, we all play at the same time and you cannot play on the server outside of the playing time. It is a collaborative save, we will all be working together. And, theoretically, we will use my proximity chat mod to use proximity voice chat.

I'd just spent a few months developing a proximity voice chat mod for it (which you can read about in my other blog post), so I was excited to try it.

In the end, I think we had around 10-15 people weekly for 10-or-so weeks. Factorio is a great game already, so it just made it even more fun to hear people whizz by on trains and catch a snippet of their conversation while you were talking to someone else about the most efficient way to route iron through the maze that was supposed to be the smelting area.

We ended the game by launching 10 rockets for each of the 10 players in the final session ^.^

Making maps

I love maps. Playing a video game is a great opportunity to map, which is why almost every time I host a Minecraft server I use Dynmap, which allows you to create a 2D and 3D map of the world that people can view on their web browser as if it were Google Maps.

It turns out that Factorio is no different, and there are plenty of mapping tools. I looked at a few and ended up using FactorioMaps, which is a fork-of-a-fork-of-a-fork-of-a-project. It lets you pipe in a game save, and generates a bunch of tiles and web files, which â€“ when viewed â€“ use Leaflet to display a nice web-based map.

The map is currently on https://server.alifeee.net/factorio/, but I can't promise it still will be by the time you're reading this. You can see examples of it in the pictures below :]

ammo

This is my favourite area in the map because at the end, you can see the ammo factory is fed by two sources of iron. One comes from the left of the screen and ends up on the right of the ammo factory. The other comes from the right and ends on the left.

In some way I think that is a great descriptor of the chaos of multiplayer, that two completely independent iron feeds end up feeding opposite ends of the same factory.

factory

This view is a mid-zoom of the main factory, which is also the spawn point of the map.

As you progress in time, you can see more miners be placed, power generation pop up, and just generally more and more stuff get piled onto the busiest area on the map.

lake

I picked this view because it starts quite pretty, and you can slowly see this nice inlet of the lake be polluted, terraformed, and finally forgotten about and surrounded in factory.

At least it's not completely covered over by the end. Reminds me of urban rivers.
